### **[OPINION] Arbitrary Repudiation of Health Claim and Doctrine of Estoppel**

📋 **Case Summary**
The Complainant seeks relief against the arbitrary rejection of a health insurance claim on grounds of a "pre-existing disease" (PED). The rejection prima facie appears untenable as the insurer previously settled a claim for the same condition in 2022 and renewed the policy without exclusions. The error in the hospital record is a rectifiable factual dispute.

🔍 **Issues**
• Whether the Insurer is **estopped** from invoking the PED clause after having previously accepted and settled a claim for the same condition.
• Whether the duty doctor's erroneous entry in the discharge summary can override the longitudinal medical history and previous claim approval.
• Whether the rejection constitutes a **"Deficiency in Service"** under Section 2(42) of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019.

📜 **Law Applicable**
*   **Section 2(42), CPA 2019**: Deficiency in service regarding arbitrary claim repudiation.
*   **Section 2(47), CPA 2019**: Unfair Trade Practice (if the insurer is found to be using "lame excuses" to deny settled risks).
*   **Regulation 4, IRDAI (Protection of Policyholders' Interests) Regulations, 2017**: Duty of insurer to act in good faith (Uberrimae Fidei).

⚖️ **Legal Analysis**
**Issue 1 (Estoppel):** The Insurer, having settled the 2022 claim, had constructive and actual knowledge of the condition. By renewing the policy without adding "PED exclusions" or "Loading", they have waived the right to deny subsequent claims for the same condition.
**Application:** The doctrine of **Promissory Estoppel** applies. Once the insurer accepts the risk with knowledge, they cannot "blow hot and cold" (*Allegans contraria non est audiendus*).
**Counter-Argument:** The Insurer will rely on the current discharge summary. However, this is neutralized by the **longitudinal record** of the 2022 approval which acts as a judicial admission of liability.

📚 **Judicial Precedents**
*   **Manmohan Nanda v. United India Assurance (2022 SC)**: Held that once an insurer has knowledge of a condition and accepts the premium, they cannot later repudiate the claim citing the same condition.
*   **Biman Krishna Bose v. United India Insurance (2001 SC)**: Established that renewal of insurance is a continuation and insurers must act fairly.

🏛️ **Procedural Aspects**
• **Maintainability**: The claim is maintainable. The Ombudsman's rejection does not bar a consumer complaint (Sec 100, CPA 2019).
• **Limitation**: 2 years from the date of repudiation letter.

🎯 **Likely Outcomes**
**A. Petitioner Succeeds**: Full claim amount + 9-12% interest + compensation for mental agony for "bad faith" rejection.
**B. Settlement**: 100% claim settlement before the Commission to avoid punitive damages.

✅ **Conclusion**
The Complainant has a compelling case grounded in the **Doctrine of Estoppel**. The Commission is likely to grant relief provided the 2022 settlement records and a hospital "Correction Certificate" are produced as evidence.
